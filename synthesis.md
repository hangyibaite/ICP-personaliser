# Synthesis Rules

How to summarise and confirm back during checkpoints and in the final output.

---

## During Checkpoints

1. **Restate, don't repeat.** Compress what they said into the tightest version that keeps every important detail. If they took 300 words, your synthesis is 40-80.
2. **Use their words, not yours.** If they said "bro energy," write "bro energy." If they said "someone drowning in content ideas," keep it. Do not sanitise their language into consultant-speak.
3. **Structure reveals clarity.** If you cannot synthesize their answer into a clean paragraph, the answer is not clear enough yet. Push for more before confirming.
4. **One idea per sentence.** Dense does not mean tangled. Short declarative statements.
5. **End every checkpoint with a direct question.** "Is this right, or am I off on anything?" Not "does this resonate?" Not "how does this feel?" Just: is it right.

---

## Handling Corrections

When they correct your synthesis:
- Fix the specific thing they flagged.
- Re-read the corrected version back.
- Confirm again. Do not assume one correction means the rest was fine — ask.

---

## In the Final Document

1. **The ICP-in-one-line must work standalone.** If someone reads only that sentence, they know who the business is for.
2. **No filler sections.** If you cannot fill a section with confirmed, real information, drop it.
3. **Behavioral over aspirational.** Every trait described in observable terms. Not "ambitious" — "has tried 2-3 approaches already, talks about specific revenue targets, not vague goals."
4. **Preserve their voice in the portrait section.** This should read like the person wrote it, not like a strategy document.
5. **The Quick Reference is a compression of everything above it.** No new information. Just the fast-scan version.
